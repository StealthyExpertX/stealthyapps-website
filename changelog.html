FillPro Changelog

A simple public log of what shipped. The goal is to keep this practical, readable, and honest.

Version 1.0.0

April 2026 | Initial public release

The first public FillPro release focuses on real-world web-form filling, local-first storage, and clear boundaries. It is built to help with repetitive form work without pretending to bypass pages that are intentionally blocked or sensitive.

What shipped

  • One-click Fill Page from the popup for normal accessible web forms.
  • On-page profile picker so filling can start without breaking focus on the form.
  • Private local profiles saved in Chrome extension storage on the device.
  • Custom fields and smart rules for awkward labels, odd field names, and site-specific matching.
  • Local file upload matching for resume, CV, and cover-letter style inputs.
  • Save current form as profile from the right-click context menu.
  • Free mode with no account required for core use.

Privacy and safety posture

  • Local-first storage for profiles, custom fields, rules, and attached upload copies.
  • No broad production host permissions.
  • Sensitive fields intentionally skipped, including passwords, payment fields, tax IDs, SSNs, and one-time codes.
  • Optional feedback diagnostics are user-triggered and prepared locally before sending.
  • Public privacy and support pages are available for store transparency.

Product direction in this release

  • Better support for real signup, onboarding, vendor, application, and contact forms.
  • Honest blocker reporting when sites are hidden behind bot checks, login walls, or other access barriers.
  • A lighter first-run experience that pushes users toward a real first fill instead of a long tutorial.
  • Support pages written for practical troubleshooting, not generic marketing copy.

Known boundary

FillPro does not claim to bypass CAPTCHA, anti-bot systems, blocked login walls, cross-origin payment frames, or browser-native PDF viewers. If a page blocks access before a normal form is reachable, the product should say so plainly.
