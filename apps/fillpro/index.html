Why people use it

Three reasons.

  • Local-only. Profiles never leave your browser.

How it works

Three steps.

  1. Step 1: Save your profile in the popup (or right-click on a form to capture it).
  2. Step 2: Add custom fields, smart rules, or local uploads if you need them.
  3. Step 3: Click Fill Page or use the keyboard shortcut. Done.

What FillPro skips

By design.

  • Passwords (use a password manager)
  • Credit card numbers, CVCs (use your browser's secure payment)
  • SSNs, tax IDs, government identifiers
  • One-time codes (2FA / OTP)
  • CAPTCHA / anti-bot challenges
  • Cross-origin payment iframes

FAQ

Common questions.

Does FillPro send my data anywhere?

No. Profiles, rules, and uploads stay in your browser. The only network call FillPro makes is to ExtensionPay (Stripe) when you choose to upgrade.

How is this different from LastPass or Dashlane?

Those are password managers that also do autofill. FillPro is autofill only and stores nothing remotely. They fill credentials; FillPro fills everything else (name, address, phone, custom fields, uploads, per-site rules).

What about Chrome's built-in autofill?

Chrome autofill is fine for basic fields but tied to your Google account, doesn't handle custom fields, can't do per-site rules, and skips many dynamic forms. FillPro covers those.

Does it work on job application sites like Workday or Greenhouse?

It works on most accessible HTML forms. Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, and similar sites work well. Workday is trickier because of its custom widgets, but smart rules handle a lot of it and the rest can still be filled manually.

Can I export my profiles?

Pro includes JSON import/export so you can move profiles between browsers or back them up.

What if I uninstall it?

Extension storage gets removed by the browser. Export first if you want to keep your profiles.
