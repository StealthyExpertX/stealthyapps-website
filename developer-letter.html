From The Developer

I like building useful tools for real workflows. FillPro came from seeing too many extensions underdeliver on speed, privacy, security, and practical day-to-day use. I wanted to build something better: fast, honest, and actually useful.

Hey, I am Karl, also known as StealthyX.

I have spent a lot of time building tools, plugins, technical workflows, and project systems in the game development space. From what I could see, there was a real gap. Too many extensions were not practical, were sloppy with privacy or security, were sluggish, or were just not built for real use. Some were even hogging RAM, which honestly blew my mind. People waste too much time filling the same kinds of forms and putting up with broken tools that should be helping far more than they do.

That is a big part of why I made FillPro. I did not want it to feel fake polished or overly magical, and I definitely did not want it to be just another junk extension on a wall of many. I genuinely care about quality, and I want to do the best I can to make things work better, faster, and more securely than some random knockoff. That is why this extension is built from the ground up. I wanted something real, something useful, and something backed by real support when a site is weird or broken. I take constructive feedback seriously because it helps me improve the extension.

I also care a lot about being straight with people. If a page is accessible and fillable, FillPro should help fill it fast and with as little friction as possible. If a site is behind a CAPTCHA, another anti-bot wall, a login gate, or a sensitive payment frame, I would rather say that clearly than leave people guessing. From my point of view, too many developers either hide the problem or act like nothing is wrong, and that feels untruthful.

At present, there are way too many products that oversell what they do, hide their limits, are not transparent, or make simple things harder than they need to be. I am absolutely a master of organized chaos, and I am usually deep in a major project, but one thing I do not want is for FillPro to underdeliver or stagnate. A good extension should keep evolving and improving over time. I want FillPro to stay useful, honest, and easy to work with.

Free use should stay actually useful. Privacy should be readable without a bunch of fog around it. Support should feel like a real person wrote it, because that is how I think it should be.

If FillPro saves you time, that is the win. If something breaks or feels off, I want the feedback. Questions like that are exactly what I want to look at when improving the product, because the point is to make it better on real forms and real workflows, not just make it look good in screenshots.

Thanks for giving it a shot.

Karl Davis
StealthyApps / StealthyExpertX

Friendly indie dev who loves making friends, learning new things every day, and building useful tools, scripts, and extensions. The Pro version helps support me and my family while I keep building toward a better future.

What I want FillPro to be

  • Fast to set up and get you to your first real use case as quickly as possible.
  • Private by default and clear about what stays local. No analytics nonsense, no user-stat harvesting, and no hidden form-data collection.
  • Helpful on messy real-world forms, not just polished demo pages. This is the first of many tools I want to build, and I believe the standard only gets better when we keep evolving and raising it.
  • Honest about why something did not work, what got blocked, what limits you are hitting, and which sensitive fields are intentionally left alone. If you want better support for a tricky case, let me know and I will look at it.
  • Backed by support from me, Stealthy. I am self-taught, I have spent years learning across different scripting and programming languages, and I take delivering on what I build seriously.
